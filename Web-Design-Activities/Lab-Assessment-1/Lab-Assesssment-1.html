Types of Computer Storage

Hard Disk Drives

Hard Disk Drives or HDDs are traditional storage devices used in personal computers. These use an electromechanical approach to data storage and retrieval. There are multiple spinning magnetic disks for this purpose.

Solid State Drives

Solid State Drives or SSDs are a type of storage device that stores data on flash memory. They are somewhat more sophisticated than HDDs as they do not have any moving parts. When someone refers to an SSD, they generally mean a SATA SSD. SATA stands for Serial Advanced Technology Attachment and currently is the market standard for connecting computers to SSDs. The interface was, however, developed for HDDs, and even though SSDs are compatible with it, it is not the best they can do.

Non-Volatile Memory Express

NVMe stands for Non-Volatile Memory Express. An NVMe SSD is a solid state drive that uses an M.2 form factor to connect to the motherboard, instead of a cable. Additionally, they use PCIe (Peripheral Component Interconnect Express) interface, which provides a much more efficient way to transfer data.
